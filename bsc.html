      BSc Syllabus and Subject Overview

      BSc syllabus and subject combines theories and practises, with an emphasis on experimentation and research. The BSc course provides students with a comprehensive and theoretical understanding of a subject, whereas the BTech course is more practical in nature.    

      Bachelor of Science has always been a popular and challenging course for students. Students have traditionally pursued a BSc in popular courses such as Physics, Chemistry, Botany, Zoology, and Mathematics. The number of BSc subjects is increasing as technology advances. Many medical-related courses are available under BSc, such as BSc Optometry, BSc Cardiology, BSc Radiology, BSc Anaesthesia, BSc Microbiology, and so on. The BSc course syllabus focuses on laying a solid foundation in science-related subjects. The BSc syllabus includes an understanding of the fundamental physiology and mechanisms of modern life. The majority of the BSc curriculum includes laboratory and research projects to help students gain a more comprehensive and global perspective. 

      BSc subjects are divided into six semesters. To pass a semester, both the theory and practical exams must be passed. Colleges are unable to provide all specialisations due to the broad range of BSc subjects available. A college typically offers 5-6 subjects in BSc Courses. The first year of BSc subjects consists of two core papers, one generic elective, and one compulsory subject. Read on to know more about the BSc Syllabus and Subject.

      BSc Course Structure

      BSc Course Structure is meticulously designed in such a way that it imparts knowledge in various domains of science. The students get to learn from basic concepts to advance aspects of science. The course structure of BSc in India is so far divided into 3 years duration which has further been segregated into six semesters in most of the colleges and universities. The first year usually in all the courses focus on brushing up the knowledge gained in XIIth examination and then building on the same. The course specialization further decides the outline of the course structure. The second year targets all the theoretical and practical aspects of the related courses in detail and is considered to be the most crucial part of the course structure. The course structure in fourth and fifth semester also allows students to choose from different choice based credit systems and domain and specialization electives. The final semester of BSc Course Structure outlines the concept of dissertation, major projects and conduct of viva voce for the students. The important highlights of BSc Course Structure are as follows. 
       

      • Six Semesters
      • Core Subjects
      • Specialization Electives and Domain Electives
      • Choice Based Credit System
      • Practical Training
      • Internship
      • Projects

      FAQs about Bachelor of Science Syllabus

      What are the best BSc colleges?

      The best colleges for BSc are Mirinda House, St. Stephens College, Kirorimal College, Hansraj College, IIT Bombay, Fergusson College, etc.

      What is the average BSc program course fee?

      The average course fee for this program varies from college to college. However, most of the colleges charge between 40000- 100000 for the course.

      Do we get a chance to do a summer internship for the BSc program?

      Yes, there are summer internships for most of the BSc program. However, some traditional courses prefer dissertation and major projects over internships.

      What is the average placement package after pusruing BSc course?

      The average placement package for BSc Program ranges from 500000- 700000 per annum. It also depends upon the area of specialization.

      Is Mathematics subject compulsory at 12th class for BSc admission?

      Mathematics is not necessary for all the programs but it is mandatory if someone wishes to do BSc Physics, Chemistry, Math or similar subjects. Mathematics is not compulsory for BSc Botany, Zoology, Biotechnology, Agriculture or other related subjects.
